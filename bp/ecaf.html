Electric charges and field

This blog has some basic concepts about Electric charges and field in Physics.

The branch of science in which we study charge of electrical effects of charged body is known as electricity.

The study of charges at rest is called static electricity. While the study of moving charge is known as current electricity.

Electric charge

Electric charge is the physical property of matter that cause it to experience a force when placed in a electromagnetic field.

Just like every body has mass (mass is amount of substance), everybody has electric charge.

SI Unit: Coulomb, C

Types of electric charges:

Two types:
  1. Negative charge, e- = -1.6 x 10-19 C
  2. Positive charge, p+ = 1.6 x 10-19 C

Sub atomic particles: electron, neutron, proton.

Sub atomic particles are breakable in (anti)quark. (anti)quark -> electron -> atom -> element

Rutherford told, proton is in the atom's nucleus and nucleus is somewhat in the centre of the atom.

Only electron can be imported and exported from the atom because it requires much more energy to manipulate the same with the proton from the nucleus.

Method of charging

Note: Only applies on insulators because conductors flow the current.

  1. By friction

    2. When two body rub on each other, they get charged because of transfer of electron.

  2. Induction

    3. When any charged body placed near the uncharged body without having touched, the uncharged body get oppositely charged.

    When any charged body touch the uncharged body, the charge of the charged body get transfered to the uncharged body.

Properties of charges

  1. Additive properties

    2. Total charge of any boddy = algebric sum of all charges which are peresent in different parts of the body.

  2. Conservation of charge

    3. Total charge of any system is always conserved.

  3. Quantisation of charge

    4. Any body contain charge is always = integral multiple of electron (or elementary charge).

    1µC = 10-6 C, number of electron = 1013

    Charge of any body doesn't depend on its velocity.
